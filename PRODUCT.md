# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Magic: The Gathering players of all skill levels who play the Commander format. They arrive casually — from Reddit, Discord, or a friend's recommendation — curious to discover their next deck. Context: on a couch with a phone, mid-game night, post-game wind-down. The job: spark inspiration and make choosing a commander feel like an adventure, not a spreadsheet task.

## Product Purpose

A ritual of discovery. The user spins a roulette, a legendary creature appears from the multiverse, and they decide whether to pursue it or banish it back to fate. Success means leaving the site already imagining the deck they'll build. The spin animation and reveal are the product.

## Brand Personality

Epic · Fun · Addictive. The feeling of opening a booster pack meets the tension of a slot machine meets the lore weight of Magic's world. Not precious, not loud — dramatic.

## Anti-references

- **EDHREC / Scryfall**: functional, data-dense, zero personality. Everything is a table or a list. Color palette: corporate gray.
- **Generic SaaS landing**: purple-blue gradient, hero screenshot, feature grid, "Supercharge your workflow." Invisible in its category.
- **Online casino**: neon kitsch, flashing lights, cheap confetti. The wrong kind of excitement.

## Design Principles

1. **The drama is the product.** Every state transition should feel like a scene change, not a component swap. The spin, the deceleration, the reveal: each is a moment.
2. **One thing at a time.** Never split the user's attention. Idle screen: one call to action. Spinning: the drum. Revealed: the card. Nothing competes.
3. **Dark earns its weight.** A dark theme that exists purely for aesthetics is lazy. The darkness here is the grimoire, the ritual space, the command zone at night. Every element must justify its place in that world.
4. **Addictive not manipulative.** The loop (spin, judge, spin again) should feel satisfying, not coercive. The exit (save and leave) should be just as clear as the repeat.
5. **The card is always the hero.** Scryfall's art is extraordinary. The UI is a frame for it, never a competitor.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Respect prefers-reduced-motion (all animations must have a reduced-motion fallback). Color identity filter must not rely on color alone (letter label inside each orb). Keyboard-navigable spin trigger and filter.
